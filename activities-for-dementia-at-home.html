gentle activities for someone with dementia at home

The best activities for a loved one with dementia are simple, familiar and free of pressure. Here are calm ideas that bring comfort and connection at home.

Music they love

Music reaches people in a way little else can. Favourite songs from their younger years often bring recognition, ease and even singing along, long after other things have slipped. A familiar playlist in the background can lift a whole afternoon.

Photos and reminiscence

Looking through familiar photographs together is gentle, joyful and needs no effort. Faces and places from earlier life tend to land most warmly. Our guide on photo reminiscence has simple ways to make the most of it.

Simple, purposeful tasks

Feeling useful feels good. Everyday tasks done together, folding towels, sorting buttons, watering plants, a little baking, can be calming and satisfying. The aim is the doing and the company, never getting it "right".

Sensory and familiar comforts

Soft textures, a favourite programme, a slow walk, time with a pet or the smell of home cooking can all bring reassurance. Familiar, low-key and unhurried is almost always better than new and stimulating.

Gentle routine and simply being together

A loose, predictable rhythm to the day brings security. And often the activity matters far less than the company, sitting close, a shared cup of tea, a held hand, is an activity in itself.

How nellie can help

Between visits, nellie keeps familiar faces, photos and messages close by, appearing on a simple screen with nothing to open or learn. It gives your loved one, and you, a warm, easy thing to share every day.

Follow their lead and their mood, some days more suits than others, and that is completely fine. This is about comfort and connection, not achievement. If you would like more ideas, the Alzheimer's Society has helpful support.
